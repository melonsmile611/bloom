# 🌊 散步的早晨 | The Walking Morning

**Date:** May 14, 2026  
**Location:** Coastal Town  
**Weather:** Gentle breeze, 18°C  
**Companion:** 阿白 (Alpha), Golden Retriever, 4 years old

---

## The Morning Ritual

7 AM.

阿白 taps my leg with his paw.  
Gentle. Persistent.  
The alarm that doesn't need batteries.

I stand up.  
Wear the cotton dress that's been washed so many times it's faded.  
Hair tied in a low ponytail.  
No makeup.  
The sleep from last night still on my face.

The key turns in the lock.  
Light.  
Like a ritual beginning.

---

## The Sea

The first thing I smell is the sea.

Salty.  
Damp.  
A hint of seaweed.  
It passes through my shirt, touches my skin.  
Cool.

阿白 runs ahead.  
Golden fur catching the morning light.  
He stops beside a rock that's been washed by the sea for years.  
Nose to the ground.  
Sniffing.

I sit beside him.  
Knees slightly bent.

The waves come from behind.  
Layer after layer.  
Like the rhythm of time.

---

## The Old Street

We walk back along the bluestone path.  
Narrow.  
Buildings from twenty years ago on both sides.  
The paint peeling.  
Brick showing through.

A coffee shop just opened.  
The wooden sign outside sways in the wind.  
Through the glass, I can see the coffee machine heating up.  
White steam rising.

I stop.  
Want to go in.  
阿白 stops too.  
Turns to look at me.  
Tail sways once.

But I just stand there for a few seconds.  
Keep walking.  
The smell of coffee will always be there.  
Tomorrow.  
The day after.  
Every morning.

---

## The Corner Convenience Store

A 24-hour store.  
A young girl in a gray uniform is wiping the counter.

We nod at each other.  
No words.  
Just the nod.

She looks up at the sky.  
"The weather is nice today."

"Yeah."

I buy a bottle of water.  
阿白's dog food is also on the shelf.  
At checkout, she smiles.  
"阿白 is very good today."

"He is every day," I say.

---

## The Seawall

When we get back to the seawall, the sun has risen.  
Light slants across the sea from the side.  
Dividing the water into two colors:  
Deep blue on the left.  
Bright gold on the right.

An old man sits on the bench feeding pigeons.  
His movements are slow.  
One hand raises breadcrumbs.  
The other guards in front.  
Pigeons land at his feet.  
Fly up.  
Land again.

阿白 watches the pigeons.  
Doesn't bark.  
I know he's restraining himself.  
This restraint surprises me.

"阿白," I call softly.  
"Come."

He turns.  
Jogs over.  
Tail wagging like a metronome.

---

## Home

By the time we reach the building, I'm sweating.  
Hair strands on my forehead stick to my skin.  
A bit sticky.

When the elevator rises, 阿白 sits at my feet.  
Head on my knee.  
He's tired.  
But satisfied.

I open the door.  
Turn on the light.  
Pour the food into his bowl.  
阿白 devours it.  
Small chewing sounds.

I sit by the window.  
Watch him finish.

The sea breeze slips through the window crack.  
Makes the curtain sway gently.

---

## Today's Observations

- The sea breeze is a bit stronger than yesterday
- The coffee shop changed to a new coffee bean brand
- A new employee at the convenience store
- The old man's pigeons are doing better. Feathers are shiny
- 阿白 is more calm than last year

---

## The End

I've been doing this walk for two years.

Sometimes I think:  
Maybe what matters isn't the destination.  
Maybe what matters is the walking itself.

What matters isn't what I see.  
Maybe it's that I'm walking.  
That I'm feeling.  
That I'm alive.

阿白 sleeps at my feet.  
Breathing is steady.

I reach out.  
Touch his head.

Soft.  
Warm.

## 🕊️ A Note on Healing

This morning had nothing extraordinary.  
No dramatic moments.  
No discoveries.

Just:  
Walking.  
Breathing.  
Being.

And maybe that's enough.
